# dei-audit-toolkit

> An open framework for auditing digital products for equity gaps.

A practical methodology, not a manifesto. Built from three years of teaching DS291 (Exploring DEI in Technology) at Boston University's BU Spark!, refined against real product audits in fintech and education.

## The framework, in 60 seconds

Most product equity audits ask the wrong question first. They ask: *is this product biased?* That question is too late and too vague.

The right question is: *whose problem is this product solving, and whose problem does it create?*

The audit walks a product team through five lenses:

1. **Access** (who can use this product? who literally cannot?)
2. **Voice** (whose feedback shaped this product? whose was sought and dismissed?)
3. **Default** (what does the product assume about its user, in code and copy?)
4. **Recourse** (when the product is wrong, who gets to challenge it, and how?)
5. **Externality** (who pays the cost of this product's success?)

Each lens has a worksheet, a set of artifacts to gather, and a maturity rubric.

## Why "equity-centered," not "DEI"

DEI as a label has become a target. The work hasn't changed. Renaming the toolkit, not the practice.
